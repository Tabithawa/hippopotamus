Hippopotamus

A Hippopotamus is a large herbivorous mammal , that lives lives in both land and water. Most people consider them dangerous but only if you invade their territories.

Below are 5 facts about hippos:

  1. Hippos are most active at night, when they forage for food. They are herbivores, and eat mostly grass and can eat upto 35kg of grass in a night.
  2. hippos are great swimmers and can hold their breath for up to five minutes underwater. When completely submerged, their ears and nostrils fold shut to keep water out.
  3. Dominant males are very protective over their group. To warn off rival males, they open their huge mouths and display their long, curved canines! They also make loud grunts and aggressive splashes in the water.
  4. Female hippos, called cows, give birth every two years, usually to a single calf. Soon after birth, the mother and her baby join up with other cows and calves for protection against predators, such as crocodiles, lions and hyenas.
